1900

Birth of the Modern Automobile

Around 1900, gasoline-powered vehicles started to gain popularity thanks to inventors like Karl Benz and Henry Ford, laying the foundation of today's automobile industry.

1910

Introduction of Mass Car Production

In 1910, the implementation of assembly line production, pioneered by Henry Ford, significantly reduced manufacturing costs and made cars more accessible to the public.

1920

Luxury and Design Take the Wheel

By the 1920s, car designs evolved beyond functionality, introducing stylish exteriors, enclosed cabins, and features for comfort, reflecting status and elegance.

1930

Rise of Aerodynamic and Streamlined Cars

The 1930s introduced aerodynamically styled vehicles, aiming for better fuel efficiency and futuristic looks, shaping modern automotive design language.

1940

Wartime Innovation and Military Vehicles

During the 1940s, the focus shifted to rugged military vehicles such as the Jeep, which later influenced post-war off-road and utility vehicles.

1977

Emergence of Compact and Efficient Cars

The oil crisis of the 1970s pushed manufacturers to develop smaller, fuel-efficient cars, changing consumer preferences globally.

This decade sparked competition in reliability, affordability, and efficiency, influencing car production worldwide.
